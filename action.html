What Can I Do?

There are plenty of opportunities that an individual can take to reduce the problems with industrial animal farming.

Eat less meat

This is by far the best option that you can do to reduce health risks. If you avoid eating meat, then meat factories will have to produce less. This will result in a healthier atmosphere and reduce the risk of human transmitted diseases from animals. In addition, eating less meat will directly impact you because it will reduce your chances at getting infected by foodborne illnesses such as salmonella.

If possible, it would be best to go vegetarian or vegan. However, this is not entirely necessary as many people are simply just choosing consciously reduce their meat intake. Some celebrities that are choosing to follow this trend are Arnold Schwarzenegger and James Cameron.

Avoid industrially farmed meat

This is also a fairly obvious option. Most of the problems that this website lists stem as a result of industrial farming practices. Methods such as scalding chickens' feathers off and cramming pigs into tight pens all increase the risk of infections spreading. Don't support companies who abide by these practices. Rather, buy meat from companies that practice free range, organic farming.

Continue educating yourself

The more you learn about meat, the more you will know when it comes to deciding your eating habits. Below are some of my favorite sources (which I also used during the making of this website)

  • Factory Farm Nation by Food & Water Watch
  • Happier Meals by Danielle Nierenberg
  • Impacts of Intensive Livestock Production... by Lidwien Smit and Dick Heederik

Join an organization

It is also highly beneficial to join an organization. While groups like FARM focus primarily on animal rights, they still are very influential when it comes to lobbying against industrial animal farming.
