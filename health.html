Foodborne Illnesses


Industrial practices have been proven to be responsible for an increase in foodborne illnesses.


Some of these illnesses, such as salmonella and e. coli are fatal and kill hundreds of people each year.


One such instance where this happened is in summer 2010 where two companies, Wright County Egg and Hillandale Farms, had to recall half a billion eggs because 1,500 people got sick from salmonella. (Read more about the incident here on page 18)


As the video on the right from the Oregonian explains, factory practices such as scalding the chicken with water to remove feathers and using machinery to clean out guts. While these methods increase productivity, they also can carry the risk of spreading harmful bacteria.


However, while the idea of dying from a piece of undercooked chicken is frightening, it does not mean you should avoid poultry entirely. Organic, non-industrial farmers defeather and gut their chickens using safer, more attentive methods. These have a smaller chance of spreading bacteria.
