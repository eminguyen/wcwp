2009

Swine Flu Outbreak

This was the most recent major animal related disease outbreak. Workers at industrial farming facilities were found to be a major link between the disease spreading from pigs to humans. To learn more about swine flu, visit our diseases page.

2002 - 2007

Massive Population Growth

According to statistics given by Food & Water Watch, some of the largest factory farms experienced an increase of 20% in population. Some other relevant numbers coming from their 2007 analysis include

  • Pig population increased by 33%
  • Cow population increased by 17%
  • Broiler chicken population nearly doubled to over one billion.

2003

Avian Flu Outbreak

Avian flu ran rampant in southeast Asia as the poultry industries in these countries grew tremendously. When the Phillipines, Malaysia, Thailand, and Vietnam began practicing industrial farming, it was no coincidence that the worst avian flu outbreak also occurred. To learn more about avian flu, visit the diseases page.

2000

Manure Outbreak

In 2000, waste products from a local farm contaminated the city's local water supply. Over a thousand people fell sick and four deaths occurred. To learn more about the risks of solid pollutants, visit the solid wastes page.

1976

FARM Founded

In 1976, the Farm Animal Rights Movement was created. This was the world's first organization dedicated to protecting animals raised for food. While this group's primary goal is to increase public adoption of veganism, the group also deals with how farmed animals are raised. In 1982, FARM's Veal Ban campaign became the first national campaign focusing on factory farm cruelty.
