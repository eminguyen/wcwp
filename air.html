Diminished Air Quality

Research has found that the expansion of livestock factory farms has had a tremendous impact on the planet's air quality. In farming communities, emissions of particles, gases, and endotoxin diminish the air's breathability. People living near these communities have been shown to be at a higher risk of developing breathing problems such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. All of these pollutants in the air are a result of the spread of manure, straw, hair, feathers, and animal food.

A Global Problem

Air pollutants do not simply affect local farming communities however. As this video explains, factory farming is responsible for the majority of the world's air problems.
